Data & Cookie Policy

Effective Date: April 13, 2026

This Data & Cookie Policy explains what data technologies Courtside uses, how your data is stored, and how it moves between your devices and any external services.

1. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

Courtside is a native iOS application. It does not use cookies, web beacons, pixels, or any browser-based tracking technologies. The App does not contain any advertising SDKs, analytics frameworks, or third-party tracking libraries.

2. On-Device Data Storage

Courtside stores all user-created data (teams, rosters, games, statistics, lineups) locally on your device using Apple's SwiftData framework, which uses an encrypted SQLite database. This data never leaves your device unless you have iCloud sync enabled (see Section 4 below).

2.1 Types of Data Stored Locally

Roster DataPlayer first/last name, jersey number, active statusTrack player participation
Game DataDate, opponent, period format, scoresRecord game outcomes
Statistical EventsStat type, period, timestamp, shot zoneLive stat tracking

3. Camera and Photo Data

When you use the roster-scanning feature, the App accesses your camera or photo library to capture or select an image of a printed roster sheet. This image is:

  • Processed on-device using Apple's Vision framework to extract text.
  • If on-device recognition is insufficient, optionally sent to Google Gemini API for a single text-extraction request.
  • Not stored by the App after processing is complete.
  • Not used for any purpose other than extracting player names and jersey numbers.

4. iCloud Sync (CloudKit)

If iCloud is enabled on your device, Courtside data may automatically sync to your other Apple devices through Apple's CloudKit service. This means:

  • Your data is encrypted in transit and at rest by Apple.
  • Data is stored in your personal iCloud account, not on our servers.
  • We have no ability to access your iCloud-synced data.
  • You can disable this by turning off iCloud for Courtside in your device's Settings > [Your Name] > iCloud.

5. Third-Party Data Transfers

The only external data transfer Courtside performs is the optional Gemini API call described in Section 3. No data is sold, rented, or shared with advertisers, data brokers, or any other third parties. The following table summarizes all data flows:

DestinationData SentWhenRequired?

6. Data Export

Courtside allows you to export game statistics as CSV files. Exported files are generated on your device and shared using the iOS share sheet. We do not receive a copy of any exported data.

7. Data Deletion

You can delete your data at any time by:

  • Deleting individual games, teams, or rosters within the App.
  • Uninstalling the App, which removes all locally stored data.
  • Managing iCloud storage through your device Settings to remove synced data.

8. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Data & Cookie Policy periodically. Changes will be posted with a revised effective date. Continued use of the App after changes constitutes acceptance.
